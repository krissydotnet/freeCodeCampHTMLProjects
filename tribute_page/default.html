Walt Disney was a man who loved to dream. This year marks the 60th birthday of Mickey Mouse. Mickey Mouse is an icon that is known around the world. But Mickey Mouse not Walt Disney's only accomplishment. Walt Disney is the man who built the place where everyone can step into a world of dreams and relive their childhood. No matter how many times I have gone to Walt Disney World, I still love that feeling of walking down Mainstreet to Cinderella's castle.

Here's a time line of Walt Disney's life

  • 1901 - Walt Disney was born in Chicago.
  • 1923 - Disney moved to Hollywood.
  • 1925 - Disney married Lillian Bounds, an ink artist.
  • 1926 - Oswald the Lucky Rabbit was created.
  • 1928 - Mickey Mouse first appeared as a single screening of the short Plane Crazy. Then Mickey Mouse appeared in Steamboat Willie becoming the first post-produced sound cartoon.
  • 1933 - Daughter Diane was born.
  • 1934 - Disney began production of Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs.
  • 1936 - Daughter Sharon was adopted.
  • 1937 - Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs premiered in December 1937 and became the most sucessful sound film at the time.
  • 1950 - Cinderella was released.
  • 1955 - Sleeping Beauty was released.
  • 1955 - Disneyland opened in California
  • 1965 - Disney announced plans to open Disney World in Orlando, FL.
  • 1966 - He died from lung cancer on December 15, 1966 ten days after his 65th birthday.
